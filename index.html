Our Stance

Health & Safety

While we were celebrating our engagement, the COVID-19 pandemic was wrecking havoc around the world. We waited a few months to see if the dust would settle. It was our hope to be able to safely have a wedding with our family, friends, and loved ones. Sadly, it does not seem like this will be a possibility any time soon. Jenna had to defend her Ph.D. via Zoom when PA's stay-at-home order caused the university to go virtual mid-semester. Ryan has been working remotely for almost 6 months as his office building is closed.

We would be lying if we said that it has been easy to make decisions about our wedding. While nothing would make us happier than celebrating our wedding with you in person, we agreed that the health and safety of our loved ones was more important than having a big in-person celebration.

Instead, we would like to invite you to join our ceremony virtually. We are having a small ceremony in a local church that's large enough for social distancing of our immediate families. We are working with the church to coordinate a live stream of the ceremony. When we can, we are looking forward to safely celebrating with you in person!

With love,

Jenna and Ryan
